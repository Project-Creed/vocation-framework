# Repository Goal

Create a practical framework for useful work and durable contribution downstream of Ethosism. The Vocation Framework applies objective reality, reciprocity, integrity, long-term responsibility, and contribution to work, craft, livelihood, enterprise, reputation, professional ethics, technology, teams, leadership, succession, and legacy.

# Writing Direction

When writing or revising this book, follow [docs/writing-guide.md](docs/writing-guide.md). Keep the Vocation Framework grounded in observable reality, reciprocal moral reasoning, standards of craft, honest value creation, professional integrity, and contribution across self, family, customers, teams, institutions, society, and future workers.

The work should be secular and non-theological: it may draw respectfully from religious, philosophical, scientific, civic, and practical traditions, but it must not depend on revelation, clergy, supernatural claims, partisan identity, or any single theology for its authority.

Prefer concrete, defensible claims over vague inspiration. Claims about work should be testable against usefulness, consequences, role reversal, quality, incentives, integrity, and decade-scale responsibility.

The repo-local Codex skill for this work lives at [.agents/skills/vocation-writing](.agents/skills/vocation-writing). Keep Vocation-specific Codex skills in this repository rather than installing them globally.

# Review Workflow

When reviewing or revising a chapter with the user, proceed paragraph by paragraph. Present one paragraph at a time, wait for the user's edits or approval, apply edits to that paragraph, then move to the next paragraph in the chapter.

Do not batch-review an entire chapter unless the user explicitly asks for a full-pass review. The default review method for this book is interactive paragraph-level editing.
