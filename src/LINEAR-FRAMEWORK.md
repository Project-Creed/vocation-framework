# Vocation Linear Framework

The Vocation Framework moves from personal capacity to durable contribution.

## Phase One: Name Useful Work

The reader begins by distinguishing vocation from status, job title, and romantic self-expression. Work is judged by usefulness, craft, responsibility, and contribution to real people.

Chapters: 01-06.

## Phase Two: Build Livelihood Without Losing Integrity

The reader then faces reputation, provision, money, markets, enterprise, ambition, and service. Livelihood matters, but it must remain answerable to real value and role reversal.

Chapters: 07-12.

## Phase Three: Produce With Others

The reader applies vocation to creativity, tools, teams, leadership, professional ethics, and failure. Useful work rarely remains solitary. It requires trustworthy collaboration and correctable production.

Chapters: 13-18.

## Phase Four: Make Work Durable

The final movement concerns sustainability, teaching, ownership, public contribution, legacy, and fulfillment. The test is whether the work strengthens people and systems beyond the worker's immediate effort.

Chapters: 19-24.

## Operating Cadence

Use this cadence when applying the framework:

1. Name the work.
2. Identify who is served.
3. Identify what quality requires.
4. Name the incentives and promises attached to the work.
5. Reverse roles with the customer, worker, teammate, apprentice, or future maintainer.
6. Choose the next act of useful production.
7. Review whether the work became more trustworthy, competent, and contributive.
